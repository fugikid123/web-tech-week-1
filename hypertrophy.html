Foundations of Muscle Growth

Master these three pillars to maximize your hypertrophy potential.

1. Progressive Overload

The gradual increase of stress placed upon the body during exercise. This can be achieved by increasing weight, repetitions, sets, or decreasing rest time.

Goal: Force the body to adapt to increasing demands over time.

2. Full Range of Motion (ROM)

Performing an exercise through the entire movement potential of a joint. Research shows that training at longer muscle lengths is superior for hypertrophy.

Goal: Maximize muscle fiber recruitment and mechanical tension.

3. Controlling the Movement

Focusing on the eccentric (lowering) phase and avoiding the use of momentum. A controlled tempo increases "Time Under Tension" (TUT) and ensures the target muscle is doing the work.

Goal: Minimize injury risk and optimize stimulus-to-fatigue ratio.
